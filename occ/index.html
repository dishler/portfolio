Project Overview

OneClick Code is a platform that simplifies the process of obtaining roofing code reports for specific addresses. OCC provide access to information on roofing codes, sales tax, manufacturer specifications, permit fees, and jurisdictional authority in one convenient location.

The Challenge

The challenge of the redesign of the OC Code project was to improve the user-friendliness of an existing platform that simplifies the process of obtaining and utilizing roofing code reports for specific addresses, making it easier for professionals in the roofing industry to navigate compliance and regulations. The goal was to create a more aesthetically pleasing and usable design, accessible on both web and native devices, with the aim of increasing the conversion of users from the Starter plan to the Pro plan.

The Solution

The design team at OneClick Code, in cooperation with the business analyst, conducted a thorough competitor analysis to understand the market and the types of products we needed to focus on. I played a key role in this process, working on information architecture, wireframing, and UI design. After implementing the design in code, we used the HotJar program to research user behavior and gain insights into how they were using the product. This helped us to make informed decisions and improve the overall user experience.

My role in the project

As an integral part of the OneClick Code (OCC) design team, my primary focus was on UX research. Starting in 2021, I collaborated closely with the business analyst and the team of developers to establish user flows for the feature that allowed users to invite and accept team members. In addition to creating wireframes and designing the UI for the native and web app, as well as the mobile web app, I played a crucial role in conducting:
  • Stakeholder interview
  • User research
  • User flows
  • Information architecture
  • Wireframes
  • Visual Design
  • Design system
  • Usability testing

Customer Journey Map

In our project, we created a Customer Journey Map to gain a better understanding of our users' experience with our product. We mapped out the different stages of the customer journey, from initial awareness to post-purchase evaluation, and identified key touchpoints and pain points. Through this activity, we discovered areas where the customer experience could be improved and identified opportunities to enhance the customer journey. Information Architecture

In the OCC project, the Information Architecture (IA) was an important aspect of the UX design process. Our team conducted a thorough analysis of the existing IA and determined the key elements that needed to be included in the new design. The main goal of our IA work was to create a clear and intuitive structure that would enable users to easily navigate and find the information they needed. To achieve this, we developed a hierarchical organization of the content and functionality, breaking down the site into categories, sections, and sub-pages. This helped us to create a clear and logical path for users to follow as they moved through the site. Additionally, we used clear and concise labeling to help users understand the purpose of each section and what they could expect to find there. The IA work was also informed by our user research and testing, which allowed us to validate our design decisions and ensure that our IA was meeting the needs of our users. As a result, we were able to create a highly effective and intuitive IA that helped users achieve their goals quickly and easily.

User Flows

The OCC project involved the creation of 40 user flows, designed for both web and native applications. Our goal was to create an intuitive and seamless experience for the users by mapping out their journey through the platform, taking into consideration their goals, pain points, and preferred method of communication. To achieve this, our team worked closely with the business analysts to understand the users' needs and developed detailed user flows for each step of the process. These user flows were tested with real users to validate our assumptions and ensure that the platform was user-friendly and efficient. The final product was a platform that not only met the users' needs but also helped professionals in the construction industry to save time, make money, and navigate compliance and regulations with ease.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

In the OneClick Code project, our team created wireframes for both native and web platforms, totaling about 250 screens. These wireframes served as a blueprint for the design and development of the platform, ensuring a seamless user experience and efficient navigation. The wireframes were carefully crafted to meet the unique needs of the roofing industry and provide a solution to the challenges of obtaining accurate, up-to-date roofing codes. The result was a comprehensive, user-friendly platform that streamlined the process of finding the right roofing codes and other information for contractors, adjusters, and insurance providers.

Results and takeaways

The OCC project aimed to create a user-friendly platform for the roofing industry. The team successfully developed a design that simplifies the process of obtaining and utilizing roofing code reports, making compliance and regulations easier to navigate. The platform was well-received by users and achieved its goal of increasing conversions from Starter to Pro plans.

Key takeaways from this project are:

  • User testing doesn't end after development. Design is a constant iteration of improving the experience for the end user. Always find ways to collect and listen to your user's feedback.
  • Involve engineering upfront. This helps to reduce any rework later on as an understanding of the technical limitations upfront will help to inform your design strategy.
