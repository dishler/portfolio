Project Overview

Amity is a B2B platform that provides remote control of stoves in living facilities for elderly and disabled individuals. Maintenance staff can monitor and adjust stove usage through the Amity web platform, while residents can control their own stoves through the Ome app. Ensuring safety and ease of living for all.

The Challenge

The challenge was to design a complex B2B platform for remote control and monitoring of stoves in living facilities for elderly and disabled individuals. The platform needed to allow for easy control of stoves by both residents through the app and maintenance staff, while balancing the need for safety, convenience, and independence. The key objective was to provide a solution that allows users to switch off, block, and monitor the status of stoves remotely.

The Solution

To meet the client's request, a comprehensive B2B platform, Amity, was designed to control stoves in living facilities for elderly and disabled individuals. The design process involved a thorough specification discovery, user flow analysis, information architecture, wireframing, and visual design (UI) to ensure an intuitive and user-friendly solution. The platform, connected to the Ome app, allows residents to control their own stoves, while maintenance staff can monitor and adjust stove usage through the Amity web platform. The end result is a solution that strikes a balance between safety, convenience, and independence for all users, providing a comfortable and secure living environment for elderly and disabled individuals in these facilities.

My role in the project

I was responsible for designing the Amity platform from the ground up. I led the specification discovery process, analyzed user flows, and created the information architecture to ensure the platform met the needs of all stakeholders. I designed wireframes and the visual user interface (UI), focusing on usability and user experience to create an intuitive and easy-to-use solution. Throughout the project, I worked closely with the development team to ensure the platform was built according to the design specifications and met the requirements of the client. My attention to detail and dedication to creating a high-quality product resulted in a platform that effectively balances safety, convenience, and independence for all users. During the Project I was working on:

  • User flows
  • Wireframes
  • Ui design

Information Architecture

As the lead designer, I was responsible for developing the information architecture (IA) of the platform. The IA process involved defining the structure and relationships between different sections of the platform, to ensure an intuitive and easy-to-use experience for all stakeholders. This involved creating a sitemap and information architecture diagram that reflected the platform's structure and user journey.

Wireframes

During the wireframing process, I collaborated closely with the client to ensure their requirements were met. The final wireframes were based on the project specifications and showcased the placement of elements, interactions, and user flows. The successful wireframing process provided a solid foundation for the platform's development and set the stage for a user-friendly and intuitive design.

Results and takeaways

The Amity project was a success, resulting in the successful launch of the MVP version of the platform. Our team worked diligently to bring the client's vision to life, and the result is a platform that is both functional and user-friendly. The platform offers robust features and capabilities, delivering on the needs of the end-users and providing a seamless experience for maintenance staff.

One of the key takeaways from this project is the importance of collaboration and open communication with the client. By working closely with the client and ensuring their input was incorporated into the design, we were able to deliver a product that exceeded their expectations. The user-centered design approach, which prioritized the needs of the end-users, also played a critical role in the success of the project.

In conclusion, the Amity project was a great success and a testament to the power of teamwork and user-centered design. The platform is now live and making a positive impact on the lives of elderly people and people with some disorders. We are proud to have been a part of this project and are excited to see what the future holds for Amity.

Key takeaways from this project are:

  • User testing doesn't end after development. Design is a constant iteration of improving the experience for the end user. Always find ways to collect and listen to your user's feedback.
  • Involve engineering upfront. This helps to reduce any rework later on as an understanding of the technical limitations upfront will help to inform your design strategy.
